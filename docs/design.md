# Design

Grounded Art is designed to be beautiful and creative while staying minimalist, so the
interface never competes with the art, galleries, and posts it presents.

## Principles

- Minimalist canvas. The interface is a quiet, gallery-like frame. Generous whitespace
  and a strong grid let the work breathe. The art supplies the color and energy.
- Bold and expressive through layout and type. Because imagery carries the visual
  weight, the interface expresses itself through confident typography, scale, and
  considered composition rather than decoration.
- Contemporary and editorial. The feel is closer to a modern art publication or an
  artist-run space than a generic listings site.
- Local without cliche. The design conveys bold, beautiful Cape Town creatives and the
  work of niche, brand new local designers. We avoid dated and on-the-nose "South
  African" defaults and tropes. Nothing about the design leans on safari, wildlife,
  flag colors, or stock heritage patterns.
- The art leads. Color, contrast, and movement come from the content. The frame stays
  restrained so featured work always reads first.

## Palette direction

A neutral base of paper and ink. The interface stays low-chroma so that gallery images
and posts carry the color. Accent is restrained and used sparingly.

## Copy

All user-facing copy reads as human-written. AI tells are stripped, including em dashes.
